<p style="margin-left:0px;"><strong>Cricket</strong> is a <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bat-and-ball_games">bat-and-ball game</a> played between two teams of eleven players each on a <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cricket_field">field</a> at the centre of which is a 22-yard (20-metre) <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cricket_pitch">pitch</a> with a <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wicket">wicket</a> at each end, each comprising two <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bail_(cricket)">bails</a> balanced on three <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stump_(cricket)">stumps</a>. The game <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Delivery_(cricket)">proceeds</a> when a player on the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fielding_(cricket)">fielding team</a>, called the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bowler_(cricket)">bowler</a>, "bowls" (propels) the ball from one end of the pitch towards the wicket at the other end, with an "<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Over_(cricket)">over</a>" being completed once they have legally done so six times. The <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Batting_(cricket)">batting side</a> has one player at each end of the pitch, with the player at the opposite end of the pitch from the bowler aiming to strike the ball with a bat. The batting side scores <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Run_(cricket)">runs</a> either when the ball reaches the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boundary_(cricket)">boundary</a> of the field, or when the two batters swap ends of the pitch, which results in one run. The fielding side's aim is to prevent run-scoring and <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dismissal_(cricket)">dismiss</a> each batter (so they are "out", and are said to have "lost their wicket"). Means of dismissal include being <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bowled">bowled</a>, when the bowled ball hits the stumps and dislodges the bails, and by the fielding side either <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caught">catching</a> a hit ball before it touches the ground, or hitting a wicket with the ball <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Run_out">before</a> a batter can cross the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crease_(cricket)">crease line</a> in front of the wicket to complete a run. When ten batters have been dismissed, the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Innings">innings</a> ends and the teams swap roles. The game is adjudicated by two <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Umpire_(cricket)">umpires</a>, aided by a <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Third_umpire">third umpire</a> and <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Match_referee">match referee</a> in international matches.</p><p style="margin-left:0px;"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Forms_of_cricket">Forms of cricket</a> range from <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Twenty20">Twenty20</a>, with each team batting for a single innings of 20 <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Over_(cricket)">overs</a> and the game generally lasting three hours, to <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Test_cricket">Test matches</a> played over five days. Traditionally cricketers play in all-white <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cricket_clothing_and_equipment">kit</a>, but in <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Limited_overs_cricket">limited overs cricket</a> they wear club or team colours. In addition to the basic kit, some players wear <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cricket_clothing_and_equipment">protective gear</a> to prevent injury caused by the ball, which is a hard, solid spheroid made of compressed <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Leather">leather</a> with a slightly raised sewn seam enclosing a <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cork_(material)">cork</a> core layered with tightly wound string.</p><p style="margin-left:0px;">The earliest reference to cricket is in <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_East_England">South East England</a> in the mid-16th century. It spread globally with the expansion of the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/British_Empire">British Empire</a>, with the first international matches in the second half of the 19th century. The game's rules, the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Laws_of_Cricket">Laws of Cricket</a>, are maintained by <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marylebone_Cricket_Club">Marylebone Cricket Club</a> (MCC) in <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/London">London</a>. The game is adjudicated by two <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Umpire_(cricket)">umpires</a>, aided by a <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Third_umpire">third umpire</a> and <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Match_referee">match referee</a> in international matches.</p><p style="margin-left:0px;"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Forms_of_cricket">Forms of cricket</a> range from <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Twenty20">Twenty20</a>, with each team batting for a single innings of 20 <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Over_(cricket)">overs</a> and the game generally lasting three hours, to <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Test_cricket">Test matches</a> played over five days.
